Become a SpeakOut Ambassador

Join a youth-led mental health advocacy movement helping students, families and communities speak up, seek support early, and connect with safe referral resources.

Ambassadors are not therapists. They are trained advocates who create awareness, support campaigns, identify people who may need help, and guide them toward professional support when necessary.

Application flow: Fill the Google Form → attend NGO interview → receive approval → print ambassador certificate.

What Ambassadors Do

✅ Reward: “SpeakOut Ambassador” +100 points
  • Promote mental health awareness on campus and online.
  • Support SpeakOut campaigns, outreaches and school programs.
  • Encourage safe conversations without judging or shaming people.
  • Refer people to MindCheck tools, clinics and trusted support channels.
  • Submit simple activity reports after campaigns or outreach work.

Who Can Apply?

  • Students, young professionals and community volunteers.
  • People passionate about mental health, youth support and service.
  • Applicants willing to complete basic orientation and follow safety rules.
  • People who can represent SpeakOut with respect and confidentiality.

Safety Rule

💡 Ambassadors provide awareness, encouragement and referral only. If someone is in immediate danger, call emergency services. Nigeria emergency number: 112.

Never promise secrecy where someone may be at risk. Protect privacy, but escalate urgent safety concerns.

Ambassador Training Checklist

Mark each section complete after reading or attending the session.

1. SpeakOut Mission & Ambassador Code Understand SpeakOut values, conduct rules and ambassador expectations.
2. Mental Health Awareness Basics Learn common signs of distress, stigma reduction and safe conversation starters.
3. Supportive Listening & Boundaries Know how to listen without diagnosing, pressuring or over-promising.
4. Referral & Emergency Escalation Use MindCheck tools, clinic resources and emergency pathways safely.
5. Campaign Reporting & Impact Tracking Submit clear reports, photos, attendance numbers and community feedback.
