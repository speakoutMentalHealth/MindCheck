This section is for SpeakOut / MindCheck volunteers, school counsellors, campus ambassadors, and community partners. Complete the short lessons below and take the quiz to show you understand our basic mental health support flow.

Lesson 1

1. How to receive someone in distress

Always start with safety and listening. Introduce yourself, tell them this is a safe space, and let them talk without interruption. Avoid judging, preaching, or promising things you cannot deliver. If they mention self-harm or danger, escalate to a senior volunteer or refer to the clinics page immediately.

Lesson 2

2. When to refer (our red flags)

Refer when you notice: (a) suicidal thoughts, (b) hallucinations or strange behaviour, (c) abuse/violence, (d) symptoms that have lasted many weeks and affect daily life. Use the Clinics & Resources page to find the nearest help. Always document the referral in your field log or app form.

Lesson 3

3. Recording & confidentiality

Every session must be recorded in a simple, secure way (date, age group, issue, action taken). Do not post a person‚Äôs story on social media without consent. Keep names private. Our reports to partners (NHRC, ministries, schools) use only aggregated data ‚Äî not personal details.
